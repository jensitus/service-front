What do these indicators mean?
RSI (Relative Strength Index)
Measures momentum on a scale from 0–100. Below 30 = oversold (potential buying opportunity), above 70 = overbought (potential selling signal). RSI 14 uses the last 14 price updates.
MA7 / MA14 / MA30
Simple Moving Average over the last 7, 14, or 30 price snapshots. When the short MA (7) crosses above the longer MA (14), it signals upward momentum (bullish crossover). When it crosses below, it signals downward pressure (bearish crossover).
MA Signal
BULLISH means MA7 > MA14 (short-term average above long-term — uptrend). BEARISH means MA7 < MA14 (short-term below long-term — downtrend).
Support & Resistance
Detected by finding local swing highs and lows in recent price history (requires 5 points on each side). Resistance is a price level the market has struggled to break above — sellers tend to appear there. Support is a level where buying tends to absorb selling pressure. R1/S1 are the nearest levels; R2/S2 are further away. These are short-term levels based on recent snapshots, not multi-day chart patterns.
